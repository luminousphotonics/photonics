Synergy With Offset Plant-Site Positioning

Our innovative lighting system is particularly well-suited for use with offset plant-site spacing, a horticultural technique known to enhance space utilization. By strategically positioning plants within the interstices of the LED array, this system maximizes multi-directional cross-illumination, optimizes photon absorption uniformity, and effectively addresses the critical issue of canopy penetration, ultimately promoting vigorous and uniform plant growth.

Offset plant-site spacing, as described by Green Thumb Gardening Secrets, involves staggering plants in successive rows such that each plant is positioned equidistant from its neighbors in all directions, forming an approximately circular optimal growing area for each plant. This approach is particularly advantageous for species like Cannabis sativa, which, due to their growth characteristics, can exhibit significant self-shading, leading to reduced light interception and diminished productivity in the lower canopy. As noted by Green Thumb Gardening Secrets, offset spacing is well-suited for "larger plants (e.g., broccoli, peppers, tomatoes) that need a lot of space and can easily shade each other out, significantly reducing productivity." This principle directly applies to Cannabis cultivation, where maximizing light exposure to the entire canopy is essential for optimal yield and cannabinoid production.

The Centered Square Number Sequence COB-SMD LED Array

The proposed lighting system utilizes a plurality of white-based, COB LEDs arranged according to a centered square number sequence, with colored SMD LED strips disposed along the frame of the modular fixtures which hold the COBs. This sequence dictates that the number of COB LEDs in each concentric square layer increases by four relative to the preceding layer (i.e., 1, 4, 8, 12, 16, ...). A control system allows for independent modulation of light intensity emitted by each concentric layer, for the purpose of maximizing uniformity in photon flux delivery. Synergistic Benefits of Offset Plant Spacing and Cross-Illumination

When used in conjunction with offset plant spacing, the centered square number sequence arrangement offers a unique advantage: maximized cross-illumination. Plants are strategically positioned within the “gaps” between the LED clusters. This configuration ensures that each plant receives light from multiple angles, effectively mitigating the self-shading that often occurs in dense canopies.

Advantages of Cross-Illumination in Cannabis Cultivation

Enhanced Canopy Penetration:
Cross-illumination significantly improves light penetration into the lower canopy. By delivering light from multiple directions, this approach minimizes shading and ensures that lower leaves receive sufficient photosynthetically active radiation (PAR), optimizing the photosynthetic processes of the plant canopy.

Uniform Photon Absorption:
Beyond simply delivering photons uniformly across the illuminated plane, the cross-illumination effect optimizes the uniformity of photon absorption by the plant canopy. This is crucial because the three-dimensional structure of the plant and the angle of incidence of light significantly affect light absorption. By illuminating the plant from all angles, this system ensures a more homogenous distribution of light absorption throughout the canopy.

Improved Growth and Yield:
Enhanced light penetration and uniform photon absorption contribute to improved photosynthetic efficiency, leading to more uniform growth, reduced internodal elongation, and potentially higher yields of both biomass and secondary metabolites, such as cannabinoids and terpenes in Cannabis [1], and flavonoids and phenolic compounds in various other plant species [2].

Conclusion:
The centered square number sequence COB-SMD LED array represents a significant advancement in horticultural lighting technology, particularly for Cannabis cultivation utilizing offset plant-site spacing. By maximizing cross-illumination and thereby optimizing photon absorption uniformity, this system effectively addresses the challenge of canopy penetration, promoting vigorous, uniform growth, and potentially maximizing yield. This innovative approach, informed by established horticultural principles, offers a compelling solution for optimizing indoor cultivation for a vast variety of plant species. Further research and empirical testing will be crucial to fully quantify the benefits and optimize the system for various plants and growing conditions.


Solving the Scalability Problem

As illustrated in these diagrams, the conventional approach of arranging standard fixed-frame fixtures in a grid presents a critical limitation: while some grow space dimensions allow for evenly spaced fixtures, others do not provide sufficient space to accommodate additional fixtures without compromising spacing. This forces growers to adopt uneven spacing to achieve optimal coverage, making it impossible to maintain consistent Photosynthetic Photon Flux Density (PPFD) targets across varying grow space dimensions.

In contrast, the centered square number sequence pattern arrangement eliminates this bottleneck. Each additional concentric square layer of modular fixtures adds only 6 inches of width to the lighting array, enabling consistent spacing across any grow space dimension. This modular scalability stands in stark contrast to conventional fixed-frame fixtures, which typically require an increase of 42 inches or more in width (as fixtures are often 42” x 42”, or more commonly, 42” x 44” in size).

Moreover, in scenarios where adding a full layer is not feasible due to less than 6 inches of available space, the intensity of the outermost layer can be adjusted to compensate. This flexibility ensures that consistent PPFD targets can still be achieved, even in constrained grow spaces. By decoupling fixture spacing from rigid dimensional constraints, this approach provides unparalleled adaptability and precision in lighting design.
