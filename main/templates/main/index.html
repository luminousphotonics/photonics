What is Lambertian emission?

            
A Lambertian emission refers to a light emission pattern where the radiance (brightness) observed from a surface is constant regardless of the viewing angle, meaning the surface appears equally bright from any direction, following Lambert's cosine law which states that the intensity of emitted light is proportional to the cosine of the angle between the viewing direction and the surface normal; essentially, a perfectly diffuse light source emits light in a Lambertian pattern.
        
        — R. Paschotta, Lambertian Emitters and Scatterers, RP Photonics Encyclopedia. [Accessed Oct. 27, 2023].

Why is achieving near-perfect Lambertian emission in a horticultural lighting system important?

As previously mentioned, a perfectly diffuse light source emits light in a Lambertian pattern. A "perfect diffuse light source" refers to a light source that evenly distributes light in all directions, creating a soft, shadowless illumination with no discernible hotspots.

In horticultural applications, this results in every part of every leaf receiving the same intensity of light, effectively optimizing photosynthetic processes.

Our system synthesizes data from various sensors, including:

  • Chlorophyll fluorometers for photosynthetic efficiency

  • Infrared thermometers for leaf surface temperature
  • Nutrient meters for EC, pH, and DO levels

This multimodal input integration allows the system to create precise light recipes tailored to real-time environmental and plant needs, ensuring optimal growth at every stage.

Energy Optimization Through Adaptive Dimming

The lighting array comprises concentric square layers of CoB lighting elements, in which separate layers are assigned specific degrees of intensity informed by our closed-form analytical physics/mathematical proof and lighting simulation software. This enables maximal uniformity and precise control of photon distribution, including targeted treatments of user-defined non-uniformity to introduce stress to the plant canopy for crop steering. Historical data gleaned from our agentic AI integration and advanced sensor technology informs the lighting system when reduced intensity suffices, autonomously adjusting the intensity while maintaining maximal uniformity. This minimizes power usage while maintaining optimized plant growth.
